No, really, they were okay.

Everyone gets so upset about them like they've ruined Star Wars but actually, they're just fine. Let's think about this for a second. Most people consider Star Wars to be a "space opera" meaning, it's a highly dramatized story that takes place in space. We've clearly got the space thing going on, so let's address the "dramatized" portion of the genre.

Most critics I know are very quick to lament the terrible melodrama and acting from the main cast, Anakin being the primary target for criticism. While he's clearly not the greatest actor of all, think carefully about his character and the terrible past from which he grew up. He was basically property until he was swept away by the Jedi before puberty, he never had contact with his mother, and when he did, she died in his arms. It's no wonder this kid had abandonment issues. Compounded on that, his lover attempts to distance herself after discovering that he's still very emotionally immature. Who can blame him? It's not the Jedi "we're going to teach you how to be an adult" academy, the conditions he grew up with emotionally stunted him. Forgive the kid for being melodramatic, he's had a terrible life.

So, he's emotionally scarred from his youth, he's emotionally abandoned by the love of his life, and he's emotionally inept thanks to the Jedi who insisted on using him as a tool to fulfil some crazy prophecy. How can you now feel bad for this guy? Yeah, he's a whiny jerk, but you would be too in his situation.

Now let's address the issues that people always bring up about the Ferengi knockoffs (Trade Federation sub-story). The entire reason the audience is even involved in the scenes about the trade negotiations is to highlight the corruption that is clearly occurring in the Republic. Through the entire trilogy, hints are dropped about the Sith and possible ties to the Senate, an impending doom builds under the nose of everyone in the movie all wildly signalled since the beginning. The whole intention of this sub-plot was to highlight the ineptitude of the Jedi and demonstrate that evil can appear from where we least expect it (a theme which clearly lines up with the turmoil Anakin is put through and the results of his turmoil). In short, the entire reason we're able to go on an epic space adventure for 3 movies is because these guys were manipulated from the beginning by the Sith. But everyone just dismisses it as some contrived plot-line without substance.

Jar-Jar. He is the character representation of the audience, the Greek chorus. Jar-jar is supposed to be the relatable character, the one that is forth-coming about his emotions. He represents us. But everyone hates him. If Jar-Jar is really just an extension of the audience, then, the audience must also be repulsed by themselves. To hate Jar-Jar is to hate yourself.
